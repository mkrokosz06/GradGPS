Executive Summary

The Problem Nobody Has Solved

Every academic planning tool in existence today requires a student's university to be a paying client before that student can access it. There is no consumer product that lets a student upload their transcript and get a clear, accurate picture of what they still need to graduate.

The Core Opportunity

The academic degree audit market is a $770M–$2.5B industry growing at 8–12% CAGR — yet it is entirely B2B, sold to universities, not students. Every incumbent (DegreeWorks, Stellic, EAB Navigate, Civitas) requires an institutional contract before a student can access their own graduation requirements. A student at a school that hasn't licensed the right software simply has no tool. That is tens of millions of students with no self-service option — and your entire market entry.

🎓

Indexes every public college course catalog in the US. Reads a student's transcript. Tells them exactly which courses they still need to take to graduate — for any major, at any school.

In Plain English

What This App Actually Does

No jargon. Here's what a student experiences.

The Simple Version

Every college student has a list of classes they need to graduate. Some are required for their major. Some are general requirements every student has to take regardless of major. Some are electives they get to choose. Most students have no easy way to see all of this in one place, checked against what they've already taken.

This app reads your transcript — the document that lists every class you've ever taken — and cross-references it against your college's official requirements. In seconds, it shows you a clear checklist: what you've completed, what you still need, and how many credits you have left. Think of it like a GPS for your degree. You tell it where you are, it shows you exactly how to get to graduation.

📄

Step 1 — Upload Your Transcript

You download your unofficial transcript from your school's student portal (the same PDF you'd send to a job application) and upload it to the app. Takes 30 seconds.

🔍

Step 2 — The App Does the Work

The app reads every class you've taken, matches it against Penn State's official degree requirements for your major, and figures out what counts toward what. No manual entry. No guessing.

✅

Step 3 — See Exactly What's Left

You get a complete picture: which requirements you've finished, which courses you still need, how many credits remain, and what your elective options are. Clear, accurate, instant.

Why Students Need This

Right now, figuring out what you need to graduate means:

  • Booking an advisor appointment (often weeks out)
  • Digging through a 400-page course catalog yourself
  • Cross-referencing your transcript manually
  • Hoping you didn't miss anything

Most students just guess and hope for the best. This app replaces all of that with a 30-second upload.

What "Standard" V1 Covers

  • Major requirements — every course your specific major requires
  • Gen-ed requirements — the courses every Penn State student must take regardless of major
  • Elective pools — categories where you choose from a list of approved courses
  • Credit hour totals — how many credits you've earned vs. how many you need

Key Technical Enabler

LLMs with structured output (Claude, GPT-4o) can convert raw catalog HTML into precise JSON requirement trees. Complex catalog logic like "choose 6 hours from Group A OR 9 hours from Group B, may not double-count with Core" is now parseable at scale. This didn't exist at production quality 3 years ago.

The Hardest Engineering Problem

Degree requirement logic is a constraint satisfaction problem. Courses can satisfy multiple requirements; the optimal assignment matters. Rules include branching conditions, exclusions, minimum grade floors, and catalog-year versioning. This is the core engine that creates the product's value and defensibility.

Data Strategy

Where the Data Comes From

The catalog index is built from public sources. No institutional permission or data licensing required.

🌐

Individual School Catalog Websites

Every accredited US institution publishes its complete course catalog publicly — all majors, all requirements, all course descriptions, prerequisites, credit hours. This is the primary raw data source.

Has: All degree requirements Has: Course descriptions Has: Prerequisites No API — must be scraped
🏛️

Common Catalog Platforms (Acalog, CourseLeaf, Watermark)

Hundreds of schools each use the same underlying catalog software. One scraper per platform covers many schools. Acalog alone powers hundreds of institutions with a consistent URL structure.

Has: Consistent structure Covers hundreds of schools per platform Requires per-platform scraper
📊

IPEDS (US Dept of Education)

Free federal API covering all ~6,000 schools that accept federal student aid. Good for institution metadata, programs offered (CIP codes), enrollment counts, and graduation rates — used to seed the catalog index.

Has: All US institutions Has: Programs by CIP code Free API No course-level requirements
🎓

College Scorecard API (US Dept of Education)

Free federal API with field-of-study data, earnings outcomes, tuition, admission rates. Used for supplementary metadata and prospective student features.

Has: Earnings by major Has: Tuition & aid data No degree requirement logic

Start with the highest-density student population, prove B2C demand, then expand the data moat and monetization surface.

1

Index the top 50–100 public universities by enrollment — this alone covers approximately 5 million students. Demonstrate that a student can upload a transcript and receive an accurate degree audit in seconds.

  • Scrape & parse catalogs for 50–100 largest public universities

Add community colleges (3.7M students) and build cross-institution transfer planning. This is the highest-pain user segment and has zero existing consumer solutions.

  • Semester-by-semester graduation path planner

After building the catalog data moat from the consumer side, offer an institutional tier to colleges and advisors. Enter the incumbent market with a pre-built data asset they can't easily replicate.

  • API and white-label product for institutional advisors
  • Full 4,000-school catalog coverage
  • Advisor workflow tools (appointment integration, early alerts)
  • Compete directly with DegreeWorks/Stellic on setup speed and data quality
  • Premium tier: AI-generated course recommendations, career outcome overlays
Defensibility

The Moat

Why this becomes harder for competitors to replicate over time.

🗄️

Catalog Data Asset

A continuously updated, structured index of every US college course catalog. Takes years and meaningful engineering investment to build. Incumbents have no incentive to build it without institutional clients.

✅

Correction Flywheel

Students flagging incorrect audit results become a correction data loop that improves parsing accuracy over time. More users → better data → better product → more users.

🔄

Transfer Graph

Articulation data (how credits transfer between schools) compounds with scale. The more school pairs we index, the more unique this data becomes.

📱

Consumer Distribution

Incumbents are structurally B2B and have no mechanism to build consumer distribution. They sell to procurement offices, not students. This channel is permanently open.

Risk Assessment

Key Risks & Mitigations

The most significant challenges and how to address them.

High Risk

Audit Accuracy Liability

Students making graduation decisions on wrong data. Mitigate with confidence scoring, clear disclaimers, and a human correction layer. Build trust incrementally; launch with "advisory tool" framing.

High Risk

Requirement Logic Complexity

Catalog rules (AND/OR trees, double-counting restrictions, min grade floors) are the hardest engineering problem. LLM parsing handles most cases but will have edge-case errors at launch. Needs robust validation pipeline.

Medium Risk

Catalog Maintenance Burden

Catalogs change every academic year. Requires continuous re-crawl infrastructure, change detection, and version management (students follow the catalog year they enrolled in, not current year).

Medium Risk

Transcript Format Variety

Every school's unofficial transcript PDF is formatted differently. LLM normalization handles most variation, but OCR quality on scanned documents may require fallback handling.

Low Risk

Legal / Web Scraping

Post-hiQ v. LinkedIn precedent (9th Circuit): scraping publicly accessible, factual data is generally not a CFAA violation. Catalog data is institutional/factual, not creative. Scrape respectfully: rate-limit, no auth-wall bypass.

Low Risk

Incumbent Response

Institutional players (DegreeWorks, Stellic) are structurally unable to build a consumer product without disrupting their own B2B model. They sell to procurement offices, not students — this channel is structurally theirs to lose.

Bottom Line

Why This Wins

The Market Gap Is Real

After decades of B2B tooling, not one company has built a consumer degree audit product. The infrastructure to build one (LLMs for catalog parsing, PDF extraction, public catalog accessibility) just matured. The window is open.

The Data Moat Is Buildable
